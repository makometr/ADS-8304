Huffman coding algorithm

  1. The input comes in order of non-increasing frequencies.
  2. The two lowest-frequency letters of the alphabet are selected, and a parent is created (the sum of the two frequencies of these “leaves”).
  3. Descendants are deleted and the parent is written in their place, the “branches” of the parent are numbered: the left branch is assigned “0”, the right “1”.
  4. Step two is repeated until the main parent, the “root,” is found.
